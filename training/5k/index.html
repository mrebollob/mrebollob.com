🏃 Sub-20 Minute 5K Plan

An 8-week training plan designed to help you break the 20-minute barrier in the 5K. Targets 3:59/km race pace with VO2max intervals, threshold work, and race-pace familiarity.

📊 Pace Chart

Type per km per mile per 400m
Easy Conversational pace (5:00–5:30/km)
Threshold 4:05 6:34 1:38
5K Pace (Goal) 3:59 6:24 1:36
VO2max / 3K Pace 3:52 6:13 1:33
Speed / 1K Pace 3:42 5:58 1:29

Weekly Structure

Mon: Easy 6–10 km | Tue: Intervals (alternating A/B) | Wed: Easy 6–8 km | Thu: Threshold / Race pace | Sat: Long run 12–16 km | Sun: Easy + strides

Warm-up: 20 min easy jog before quality sessions
Cool-down: 10 min easy jog

🏁 Race Strategy

  • Km 1: ~4:02 — controlled, resist going out too fast
  • Km 2–4: Lock into 3:58–4:00/km
  • Km 5: Go all-in — leave nothing on the course
  • 400m splits: ~1:36 each

Starting too fast is the #1 sub-20 killer. Trust your training and run even splits.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Running intervals too fast — pace them correctly, these aren't sprints
  • Skipping threshold work — this is what raises your ceiling
  • Not practicing race pace — if 3:59/km feels foreign, it will on race day
  • Easy runs should be genuinely easy — 5:00–5:30/km is correct
